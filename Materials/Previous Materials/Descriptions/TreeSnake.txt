Habitats: The common tree snake, Dendrelaphis punctulatus, (also called green tree snake and Australian tree snake) is a slender, large-eyed, non-venomous, diurnal snake of many parts of Australia, especially in the northern and eastern coastal areas, and into Papua New Guinea.
Scale: Lengths in excess of 2 metres have been recorded, though 1.5-1.8 metres is a more common size.
Status: Non-Venemous
